What is SUV?

SUV or Sport Utility Vehicle refers to the four wheels vehicle that has the potential to go both on and off the road. To many people, an SUV means the smaller pick-up truck that we use to transport materials from one place to another. In a broader sense, the definition of an SUV is much more than that. Any vehicle that has the potential to go on and off the road can mark as an SUV. In that sense, a vehicle with two wheels can also be called an SUV.

The definition of SUV varies from person to person. If you are interested in learning the basics of SUVs, this article will provide everything you need to know. Let us begin with the history of SUV- SUVs were not SUVs until 1984. The first modern SUV came out in 1984, which was the Jeep Cherokee (XJ). Before that, the only four-wheel vehicles were the station wagons and carryalls that we saw in the old movies. All these vehicles could not run off-road, making them illegible to consider as an SUV. SUVs were not that much popular until 2000. As SUVs are becoming more powerful in recent days, the popularity of the category is also increasing constantly.

The most basic kinds of SUVs have the potential to go both on and off the road. Some of the SUVs might have less power to run on rough terrain, but most of them can run smoothly off the road. The chassis of the modern-day SUV will have unibody construction, making the category almost universal. In some cases, the chassis construction of an SUV can be on-body too. Almost in all SUVs, the engine compartment will be in the front, and the passenger compartment will be in the back of the car.

Though the earlier SUVs had two-door designs, the most recent SUVs tend to have four-door designs. Some of the modern days SUV can also use a four-door design. SUVs have lower fuel efficiency rates, making the environment vulnerable. Some modern days SUVs come with a higher fuel-efficiency rate. With the progression of the days, SUVs are going to be more environmentally friendly.

Types of SUVs

Mini Sport Utility Vehicle

These are the smallest SUVs on the road. People use Mini SUVs for recreational purposes or for going to a smaller distance. Although Mini SUVs are not that popular in the United States, Asian loves driving mini SUVs. Two examples of mini SUVs are the Suzuki Jimny and Lada Niva.

Compact Sport Utility Vehicle.

Compact SUVs are also smaller but they tend to be a little larger than mini SUVs. Compact SUVs tend to come with the same two-box and four-door design. They may not show enough efficiency to ride on the off-road terrain. Suzuki and Chevrolet are producing quality compact-size SUVs for a long time.

Mid-size Sport Utility Vehicle.

Mid-size SUVs are popular passenger vehicles all over the world. Most of the small size pick-up truck falls in this category. Besides, mid-size SUVs also come with conventional SUV characteristics. Toyota and Mitsubishi are producing quality mid-size SUVs for a long time. Most of the Pajero cars that you notice on the street fall in this category.

If you are looking for a spacious room inside the car, choosing an extended SUV ought to be the appropriate decision. These are the most heavy-duty and stylish SUVs that you would notice on the street. Ford, Chevrolet, and many other popular manufacturers are producing extended SUVs. On top of that, it is also possible to notice commercial SUVs that come with an extended length size.
