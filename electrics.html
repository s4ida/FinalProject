What is Electric cars?

As a result of the technology age, vehicles with engines that save fuel, do not consume oil and operate quietly and with high efficiency have been designed. Electric means move by means of a rotor. The electric vehicle works thanks to the part called the rotor, which is in the electric motor and rotates and moves. As the rotor rotates, electric current is transmitted, electrical energy transforms into motion energy. Unlike gasoline vehicles, the engine does not need to reach a certain speed in order to feel the torque in the electric vehicle. The electric vehicle is used thanks to the battery owned in addition to the engine.

Electric cars work with an engine technology that offers new experiences in terms of fuel efficiency, environmental awareness, and performance. Familiar with most electronics from washing machines to small fans, these electric motors emerge as part of a more efficient working principle in automobiles. Unlike the internal combustion engines like the classic cars running on gasoline or diesel, it has high efficiency, non-wear, and bi-directional operation features. The efficiency rate of internal combustion engines using fossil fuels is very low. Approximately 35% of the energy generated due to the moving parts and other parts in the engine produce the power to move it. This is considered to be a low rate compared to an electric car. While the motion energy is generated in internal combustion engines, high heat, noise, and vibration are generated. Electric cars, on the other hand, run quietly.

Similar to charging cell phones at home, electric vehicles can also be charged. As in mobile phones, lithium-ion batteries are used in electric vehicles. The electric vehicle can be charged within 8–10 hours by using standard sockets that provide alternating current at home. Since the electrical installation in the house is single-phase and low-amp, the charging time of the vehicle is long. Unlike homes, the vehicle can be charged in a much shorter time thanks to the high current obtained from free charging stations installed in various locations. It is possible to come across charging stations in shopping malls and gas stations. The vehicle is charged within 30–60 minutes by using the direct current obtained by the said fast-charging stations. It is known that many electric cars are charged 80% within 30 minutes. However, using an electric current with normal speed, the vehicle can be fully charged within 2 or 4 hours.

The distance traveled by an electric car with a fully charged battery varies between 150 and 600 kilometers, depending on the model of the car. Many electric car companies give the battery of the electric vehicle a minimum of 8 years and a maximum of 15 years of warranty. Although the wear of the battery varies with how often the car is used, weakening of the battery is observed after 50,000 kilometers. This situation can be compared to the wear of internal combustion engines as the distance traveled increases.

Types of Electric cars

Hatchbacks

Spacious and versatile, these family-friendly options like the Volkswagen ID.3 and Nissan Leaf offer ample room for groceries, gear, and even little ones. With their practical hatchback design and extended range, they’re perfect for weekend getaways and everyday errands, combining eco-consciousness with everyday practicality.

Performance EVs

Unleash your inner speed demon with high-performance beasts like the Tesla Model S Plaid and Porsche Taycan. These lightning-fast machines boast blistering acceleration, sleek aerodynamic designs, and mind-blowing handling, taking the concept of electric thrills to a whole new level.

SUVs and Crossovers

For the adventurous souls who crave both comfort and capability, electric SUVs and crossovers like the Ford Mustang Mach-E and Hyundai Kona Electric are the perfect companions. Combining rugged exteriors with spacious interiors and impressive range, they tackle rough terrain with ease, taking your eco-friendly explorations further than ever before.

Luxury EVs

Indulge in unparalleled comfort and cutting-edge technology with electric luxury cars like the Audi e-tron GT and Mercedes-Benz EQS. These premium vehicles offer opulent interiors, whisper-quiet rides, and advanced features that redefine the meaning of electric sophistication.

Sedans

For those who value elegance and efficiency, electric sedans like the Tesla Model 3 and BMW i4 offer a smooth and stylish ride. With sleek designs, extended ranges, and advanced driver-assistance features, they seamlessly blend luxury with practicality, making every journey a truly electrifying experience.
